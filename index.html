Gin Mixers

The perfect companions for your gin experience

Classic Indian Tonic

Best with: London Dry Gins (Gordon's, Tanqueray)

Garnish: Lime wedge, juniper berries

Recipe: 50ml gin, 150ml tonic, plenty of ice

Mediterranean Tonic

Best with: Citrus-forward gins (Bombay Sapphire)

Garnish: Lemon peel, rosemary sprig

Recipe: 50ml gin, 150ml tonic, ice, gently stir

Elderflower Tonic

Best with: Floral gins (Hendrick's, Bloom)

Garnish: Cucumber slice, edible flowers

Recipe: 50ml gin, 100ml tonic, 20ml elderflower cordial

Spiced Tonic

Best with: Spiced gins (Opihr, Whitley Neill)

Garnish: Orange slice, star anise

Recipe: 50ml gin, 150ml tonic, dash of orange bitters

Botanicals

The essential ingredients that define gin's character

🌲

Juniper Berries

The essential botanical that defines gin. Provides piney, resinous notes and is required by law.

🍊

Citrus Peels

Orange, lemon, and grapefruit peels add bright, zesty notes to balance juniper.

🌿

Coriander Seed

Adds spicy, citrusy notes and is the second most common botanical in gin.

🌸

Angelica Root

Adds earthy, musky notes and helps bind other flavors together.

🍃

Orris Root

Provides a floral, violet-like aroma and acts as a fixative for other botanicals.

🌶️

Cassia Bark

Similar to cinnamon, adds warmth and spicy sweetness to the gin profile.

🍓

Liquorice Root

Adds sweetness and a distinctive anise-like flavor to the gin.

🌼

Chamomile

Adds delicate floral notes and a soothing, honey-like character.
