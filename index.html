The History of Gin

Origins of Gin

Gin originated in the Netherlands in the 17th century as "genever," a medicinal spirit. British soldiers discovered it during the Thirty Years' War and brought it back to England.

Gin Production

Gin is created by distilling neutral grain alcohol with juniper berries and other botanicals. The distillation process allows the flavors of the botanicals to infuse into the spirit.

The Gin Distillation Process

This animation illustrates the traditional copper pot distillation process used to create premium gin. Botanicals are steeped in neutral spirit before distillation to extract their flavors.

Tonic Water Guide

The perfect tonic can elevate your gin experience. Discover the different varieties.

Classic Tonic

Traditional tonic with balanced bitterness and sweetness. Pairs well with London Dry gins.

Best with: Tanqueray, Beefeater, Gordon's

Light Tonic

Lower in sugar with subtle flavors. Allows the gin's botanicals to shine through.

Best with: Premium gins like Hendrick's, Botanist

Flavored Tonic

Infused with fruits, herbs or spices. Adds an extra dimension to your G&T.

Best with: Citrus-forward gins, floral gins

Apple Tonic

Crisp apple flavors complement botanical gins. Refreshing and slightly sweet.

Best with: Floral gins, herbaceous gins

Spiced Tonic

Gentle heat with warming spices. Perfect for complex gins with spice notes.

Best with: Barrel-aged gins, spiced gins

Mineral Tonic

High mineral content enhances gin flavors. Crisp and effervescent.

Best with: All gins, especially citrus-forward

Tonic Pairing Recommendations

London Dry Gin

Classic tonic with a slice of lime. The bitterness complements the juniper-forward profile.

Citrus-forward Gin

Elderflower tonic with a grapefruit twist. Enhances the citrus notes.

Floral Gin

Light tonic with cucumber or rose petals. Doesn't overpower delicate flavors.

Herbaceous Gin

Spiced tonic with a rosemary garnish. Complements savory botanicals.

Barrel-aged Gin

Mineral tonic with an orange twist. Balances the oak and spice notes.

Coastal Gin

Light tonic with a seaweed garnish. Enhances maritime botanicals.
