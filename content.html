Tonic Sommelier

The Perfect Pairing

Just as important as the gin, the tonic water can make or break your G&T. Our tonic sommelier guides you through the world of premium tonics, from classic Indian tonic to exotic botanical infusions.

Tonic Wisdom

"The best tonic should complement, not overpower. Look for natural quinine, balanced sweetness, and bubbles that persist." - Master Tonic Sommelier

Pro Tip

Always pour tonic over ice first to preserve carbonation. Gently stir to combine with gin - never shake, as it breaks down the delicate botanicals.

Gin Cocktail Recipes

Classic Gin & Tonic

Recommended Gin: London Dry (e.g., Beefeater)

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 150ml Premium Tonic Water
  • Lime wedge
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Fill a highball glass with ice cubes.
  2. Pour gin over the ice.
  3. Top with tonic water.
  4. Gently stir and garnish with a lime wedge.

Negroni

Recommended Gin: London Dry (e.g., Tanqueray)

Ingredients

  • 30ml Gin
  • 30ml Campari
  • 30ml Sweet Vermouth
  • Orange peel for garnish
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Fill a mixing glass with ice.
  2. Add gin, Campari, and vermouth.
  3. Stir until well chilled.
  4. Strain into a rocks glass with ice.
  5. Garnish with an orange peel.

Aviation

Recommended Gin: Floral Gin (e.g., The Botanist)

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 15ml Maraschino Liqueur
  • 15ml Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 5ml Crème de Violette
  • Cherry for garnish

Steps

  1. Add all ingredients into a shaker with ice.
  2. Shake well and strain into a chilled cocktail glass.
  3. Garnish with a cherry.

Tom Collins

Recommended Gin: Citrus-forward (e.g., Tanqueray No. TEN)

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 25ml Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 15ml Simple Syrup
  • Soda Water
  • Lemon slice and cherry for garnish

Steps

  1. Add gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup into a shaker with ice.
  2. Shake well and strain into a Collins glass filled with ice.
  3. Top with soda water.
  4. Garnish with a lemon slice and cherry.

French 75

Recommended Gin: Dry Gin (e.g., Sipsmith)

Ingredients

  • 30ml Gin
  • 15ml Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 10ml Simple Syrup
  • Champagne or Sparkling Wine
  • Lemon twist for garnish

Steps

  1. Shake gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup with ice.
  2. Strain into a champagne flute.
  3. Top with champagne.
  4. Garnish with a lemon twist.

Southside

Recommended Gin: Classic London Dry (e.g., Beefeater)

Ingredients

  • 60ml Gin
  • 30ml Fresh Lime Juice
  • 20ml Simple Syrup
  • 6-8 Mint Leaves
  • Mint sprig for garnish

Steps

  1. Muddle mint leaves in a shaker.
  2. Add gin, lime juice, simple syrup, and ice.
  3. Shake well and double strain into a chilled coupe glass.
  4. Garnish with a mint sprig.

Bramble

Recommended Gin: Berry-friendly (e.g., Hendrick's)

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 25ml Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 15ml Simple Syrup
  • 15ml Crème de Mûre
  • Fresh blackberries for garnish

Steps

  1. Shake gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup with ice.
  2. Strain into a rocks glass filled with crushed ice.
  3. Drizzle Crème de Mûre over the top.
  4. Garnish with fresh blackberries.

Gin Fizz

Recommended Gin: Citrus-forward (e.g., Tanqueray No. TEN)

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 30ml Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 15ml Simple Syrup
  • 1 Egg White
  • Soda Water

Steps

  1. Add gin, lemon juice, simple syrup, and egg white to a shaker without ice.
  2. Dry shake vigorously for 15 seconds.
  3. Add ice and shake again until chilled.
  4. Strain into a highball glass and top with soda water.
