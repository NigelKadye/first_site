Tonic Sommelier

Just as important as the gin, the tonic water can make or break your G&T. Our tonic sommelier guides you through the world of premium tonics.

Juniper Forward

Classic tonic with crisp quinine. Pairs perfectly with traditional London Dry gins.

Citrus Burst

Zesty tonic with lemon and lime notes. Complements citrus-forward gins.

Floral Notes

Elderflower or rose-infused tonic. Perfect with floral and delicate gins.

Tonic Wisdom

"The best tonic should complement, not overpower. Look for natural quinine, balanced sweetness, and persistent bubbles."

Pro Tip

Always pour tonic over ice first to preserve carbonation. Gently stir to combine with gin.

Signature Gin Cocktails

Classic Gin & Tonic

Recommended: London Dry Gin

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 150ml Premium Tonic
  • Lime wedge
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Fill glass with ice
  2. Add gin
  3. Top with tonic
  4. Garnish with lime

Negroni

Recommended: Bold Gin

Ingredients

  • 30ml Gin
  • 30ml Campari
  • 30ml Vermouth
  • Orange peel

Steps

  1. Mix all ingredients
  2. Stir with ice
  3. Strain into glass
  4. Garnish with orange

Aviation

Recommended: Floral Gin

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 15ml Maraschino
  • 15ml Lemon juice
  • 5ml Violette

Steps

  1. Shake all ingredients
  2. Strain into glass
  3. Garnish with cherry

French 75

Recommended: Citrus Gin

Ingredients

  • 30ml Gin
  • 15ml Lemon juice
  • 10ml Syrup
  • Champagne

Steps

  1. Shake gin, lemon, syrup
  2. Strain into flute
  3. Top with champagne
  4. Garnish with lemon

Bramble

Recommended: Berry Gin

Ingredients

  • 50ml Gin
  • 25ml Lemon juice
  • 15ml Syrup
  • 15ml Mûre

Steps

  1. Shake gin, lemon, syrup
  2. Pour over crushed ice
  3. Drizzle Mûre
  4. Garnish with berries

Southside

Recommended: Classic Gin

Ingredients

  • 60ml Gin
  • 30ml Lime juice
  • 20ml Syrup
  • Mint leaves

Steps

  1. Muddle mint
  2. Add other ingredients
  3. Shake well
  4. Double strain
