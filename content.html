Tonic Sommelier

The Perfect Pairing

Just as important as the gin, the tonic water can make or break your G&T. Our tonic sommelier guides you through the world of premium tonics, from classic Indian tonic to exotic botanical infusions.

Tonic Wisdom

"The best tonic should complement, not overpower. Look for natural quinine, balanced sweetness, and bubbles that persist." - Master Tonic Sommelier

Pro Tip

Always pour tonic over ice first to preserve carbonation. Gently stir to combine with gin - never shake, as it breaks down the delicate botanicals.

Perfect Pairings

Citrus Gins

Pair with Mediterranean tonic and a twist of grapefruit or lime. Garnish with rosemary or thyme for an herbal touch.

Floral Gins

Elderflower tonic complements these delicate gins. Add edible flowers or cucumber slices for a refreshing finish.

Spiced Gins

Pair with classic Indian tonic and a slice of orange. A dash of pink peppercorns enhances the spice notes.
